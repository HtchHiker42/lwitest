What We Do

The 12 Languages Initiative

Our flagship project documents and works toward revitalizing twelve endangered languages — two already extinct, others clinging to survival. We believe no voice, no matter how small, should be destroyed.

We build teams of youth researchers, documenters, and writers paired with scholarly experts to create a long-term record of these languages for all of posterity.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Can I get an official position or publish with LWI?

Yes — both high school students and scholars can hold official positions. Students may apply for research documentation roles, magazine staff positions, or leadership/board roles. All published materials carry proper author credits and are shared publicly.

Is the documentation just research papers?

Not at all. Documentation encompasses written research, multimedia recordings, digital tools, lexicons, grammar guides, and more. We aim to build a full toolkit so that anyone could learn or study these languages from our resources.

How can I support if I don't want to research or document?

You can support through our magazine as a writer or editor, through our social media and outreach efforts, by attending and sharing our events, or through direct donations to fund our upcoming conference.

Why "Lost Worlds"?

Each language carries within it an entire world — a unique way of seeing time, nature, relationships, and existence. When a language vanishes, that world is lost too. Our name reflects both what we're fighting to prevent and the urgency of the mission.
