Milestones

What We've Accomplished

🌍

Launched the 12 Languages Initiative

Selected and began comprehensive documentation work on 12 endangered and extinct languages spanning every inhabited continent — from the Amazon to Australia, the American Southwest to the Syrian highlands.

👥

Built a Growing Research Team

Recruited, onboarded, and set to work over 100 youth researchers — high school and undergraduate students — matched with languages based on their interests and backgrounds.

📚

Established Scholar Partnerships

Built formal relationships with academic advisors in linguistics, computational linguistics, indigenous studies, and native culture who provide mentorship, review, and credibility to our youth-led research.

📰

Launched LWI Magazine

Created and launched a student-written publication covering endangered languages, native cultures, LWI events, and the broader landscape of language preservation — giving our student writers a public voice.

🎙️

Hosted Student × Scholar Forums

Ran a series of live sessions connecting students directly with field linguists, allowing hands-on learning about specific languages and the methodologies used to document and revitalize them.

🗓️

Planning Our Inaugural Conference

Organizing a first-of-its-kind conference on linguistics, endangered languages, and youth-led preservation — bringing scholars, students, native community members, and the public together.

Why It Matters

The Global Language Crisis

The world is losing languages at a pace that has no historical precedent. Linguists estimate that of the roughly 7,000 languages spoken today, half will be gone by century's end — one every two weeks.

With each language lost, irreplaceable knowledge disappears — ecological knowledge encoded in words for plants and animals, mathematical concepts that don't exist in dominant languages, oral histories stretching back millennia.

LWI is building an archive of these voices so that whatever happens, something remains — and wherever possible, the language can live again.
