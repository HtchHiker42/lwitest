Lost Worlds Institute is a youth-led nonprofit born from a simple belief: that every language lost is a civilization erased, and that it doesn't have to be that way.

No voice, no matter how small, should be destroyed. We are here to make sure it isn't.

How We Began

The Lost Worlds Institute was founded by a group of high school students who found themselves captivated by languages — not just modern ones, but the ancient, the rare, and the endangered. They discovered that across the globe, hundreds of languages were slipping into silence with little more than a whisper.

What began as curiosity became conviction. If no major institution was mobilizing the energy of young researchers to document these languages, LWI would build that institution themselves — partnering with scholars, linguists, and native communities to create lasting records.

Today, LWI operates as a growing coalition of youth researchers, documentary writers, and language enthusiasts, supported by academic advisors and guided by the communities whose languages we seek to preserve. We are not just observing a crisis — we are acting to reverse it.
