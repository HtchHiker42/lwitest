Chamicuro
Chamicuran • Peru
Critically Endangered

One of the most endangered languages in the Americas, Chamicuro is spoken by a tiny community in the Peruvian Amazon. It is a language isolate within the broader Amazonian linguistic landscape, possessing unusual phonological features and a rich oral tradition.

RegionPeru (Amazon)
Speakers~8
ScriptLatin (modern)
Taa (ǃXóõ)
Tuu • Botswana / Namibia
Endangered

Taa is renowned for having one of the largest phoneme inventories of any known language, with over 100 sounds including an extraordinary range of click consonants. Spoken by the ǀXam and related San people, it represents a linguistic treasure of unparalleled complexity.

RegionBotswana, Namibia
Speakers~2,500
ScriptLatin + click notation
Yuchi
Language Isolate • Oklahoma, USA
Critically Endangered

Yuchi is a language isolate spoken only by members of the Yuchi Tribe in Oklahoma. With fewer than 5 fluent native speakers remaining, all elderly, Yuchi is among the most urgent preservation cases in North America. Active revitalization efforts are underway.

RegionOklahoma, USA
Speakers<5 fluent
FamilyIsolate
Warlpiri
Ngarrkic • Northern Australia
Vulnerable

Warlpiri is one of the larger Australian Aboriginal languages, known for its complex system of grammatical cases and a notable phenomenon of children creating a new mixed language called "Light Warlpiri." Despite having several thousand speakers, it faces pressures from English dominance.

RegionNorthern Territory, AUS
Speakers~2,000–3,000
ScriptLatin
Manx
Celtic (Goidelic) • Isle of Man
Revitalizing

Manx is a Celtic language that became extinct in 1974 with the death of its last native speaker, Ned Maddrell — and was then revived. Today, a small but growing community speaks Manx, with immersion schooling programs producing new generations of speakers. A remarkable revitalization success story.

RegionIsle of Man
Speakers~2,000+
StatusRevived
Western Aramaic
Semitic • Syria
Critically Endangered

Western Aramaic is the ancient language once spoken across the Near East — and the language Jesus of Nazareth is believed to have spoken. Today it survives only in the Syrian village of Maaloula, where a small community of speakers keeps alive one of history's most consequential languages.

RegionSyria (Maaloula)
Speakers~8,000
ScriptAramaic / Syriac
Akkadian
Semitic • Ancient Mesopotamia
Extinct

Akkadian was the lingua franca of the ancient Near East for over 1,500 years — the language of Hammurabi's Code, the Epic of Gilgamesh, and diplomatic correspondence across empires. Written in cuneiform on clay tablets, it is preserved in hundreds of thousands of surviving texts.

RegionMesopotamia (Iraq)
Died~100 CE
ScriptCuneiform
Cherokee
Iroquoian • North America
Endangered

Cherokee is unique in that it has its own syllabary, invented by Sequoyah in 1820 — one of the few times in recorded history that an individual created a complete writing system. Despite having a tribal enrollment of 370,000+, only a few thousand are fluent speakers today.

RegionOklahoma / NC, USA
Speakers~2,000 fluent
ScriptCherokee syllabary (ꮃ)
Coptic
Afro-Asiatic • Egypt
Liturgical Only

Coptic is the final phase of the ancient Egyptian language — the tongue of the pharaohs, spoken through millennia. While no longer spoken as a daily language, it survives in the Coptic Orthodox Church as a liturgical language, maintained by millions of Egyptian Christians.

RegionEgypt
StatusLiturgical
ScriptCoptic alphabet
Wampanoag
Algonquian • Massachusetts, USA
Revitalizing

Wampanoag (Wôpanâak) had no living speakers for over 100 years. Linguist Jessie Little Doe Baird led a remarkable revival effort, reconstructing the language from colonial-era manuscripts. Today, her daughter is among the first new native speakers in generations — a story of extraordinary dedication.

RegionMassachusetts, USA
StatusRevived (2+ speakers)
FamilyAlgonquian
Serrano
Uto-Aztecan • California, USA
Critically Endangered

Serrano is a Uto-Aztecan language of the San Bernardino Mountains, spoken by the Serrano people of inland Southern California. With very few remaining speakers, the Serrano people are actively working on documentation and revitalization efforts to keep their language alive.

RegionCalifornia, USA
Speakers<10
FamilyUto-Aztecan
Muscogee (Creek)
Muskogean • Oklahoma, USA
Endangered

Muscogee is the language of the Muscogee (Creek) Nation, one of the Five Civilized Tribes. It has a rich oral tradition, polysynthetic grammar, and an active community working on its preservation. The Muscogee Nation has invested in language programs, immersion schools, and digital resources.

RegionOklahoma, USA
Speakers~5,000
FamilyMuskogean
