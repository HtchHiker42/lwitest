Why We Exist

The Mission
That Drives
Everything

We exist to ensure that no language — no matter how few its speakers, how distant its homeland — disappears without a trace.

Our Beliefs

What we stand for

01

Every language is a unique lens on the human experience — encoding ways of thinking, feeling, and knowing that exist nowhere else on Earth.

02

When a language dies, a world is erased — its stories, its science, its names for colors and kinship and the stars. This is irreversible.

03

Young people have the energy, the urgency, and the moral clarity to act — and with the right scholarly support, they can produce rigorous, lasting work.

04

Preservation without community is incomplete. We partner with native speakers and organizations, never working over them or without them.

05

Documentation is not the end. Where possible, we contribute to revitalization — helping extinct or near-extinct languages gain new speakers and new life.

How We Work

Three Pillars of Preservation

📜

Documentation

Building comprehensive records of each language — phonology, morphology, syntax, lexicons, oral traditions, and cultural context — so they survive even if their last speakers do not.

🔬

Research

Producing scholarly-grade research on endangered language structure, history, and sociolinguistic context. Youth researchers publish alongside academic mentors, creating work that enters the permanent record.

🌱

Revitalization

Where communities want it, helping rebuild speaker populations through educational tools, learning materials, and partnerships with native organizations committed to language renewal.

Youth-Led, Scholar-Guided

Our model pairs high school and undergraduate researchers with expert linguists and academics. Students do the research — real, publishable work — under the mentorship of people who have spent careers in the field.

This isn't a simulation. It isn't a club activity. It's an actual contribution to the permanent linguistic record, credited under real authors, distributed to the academic community and the public.

We also operate with the full partnership of native communities. We never document a language over the objections of its speakers. We work with them, not above them.

Roadmap

Long-Term Vision

LWI is built for permanence. Our goal is not just to document 12 languages — it's to build a model for youth-led language preservation that can be replicated, scaled, and sustained for generations.
